📚 Study Notes & Quick Revision

⚙️ Mechanisms at a Glance
🦠 Antibiotic Targets
  • Cell Wall: Beta-lactams, Glycopeptides (vancomycin)
  • 30S Ribosome: Aminoglycosides, Tetracyclines
  • 50S Ribosome: Macrolides, Clindamycin, Chloramphenicol, Linezolid
  • DNA Gyrase/Topo IV: Fluoroquinolones
  • RNA Polymerase: Rifampin
  • Folate Pathway: Sulfonamides (DHPS), TMP (DHFR)
  • Cell Membrane: Polymyxins, Daptomycin
❤️ Cardiac Drug Mechanisms
  • Beta-blockers: ↓ HR, ↓ contractility (β1), bronchospasm risk (β2)
  • CCB Dihydropyridines (amlodipine): Vascular selectivity → vasodilation
  • CCB Non-DHP (verapamil, diltiazem): ↓ HR + ↓ contractility
  • Digoxin: Na/K-ATPase inhibition → ↑ Ca²⁺ → ↑ inotropy; vagal ↑ → ↓ HR
  • Amiodarone: Class III — K⁺ channel block, QT prolongation
  • Nitroglycerin: → NO → ↑ cGMP → venodilation (preload ↓)
🧬 CNS Drug Mechanisms
  • SSRIs: Block SERT → ↑ synaptic serotonin
  • SNRIs: Block SERT + NET → ↑ serotonin + norepinephrine
  • TCAs: Block SERT, NET, muscarinic, H1, α1 (↓ BP)
  • MAOIs: Inhibit MAO-A/B → ↑ all monoamines
  • Benzodiazepines: ↑ GABA-A frequency of Cl⁻ channel opening
  • Barbiturates: ↑ GABA-A duration of Cl⁻ channel opening
  • Antipsychotics: D2 receptor block → EPS, hyperprolactinemia
💉 Antidiabetic Mechanisms
  • Metformin: ↓ hepatic gluconeogenesis (AMPK); first-line T2DM
  • Sulfonylureas: Close K⁺-ATP channels → depolarize → insulin ↑; hypoglycemia risk
  • GLP-1 agonists: Glucose-dependent insulin ↑, glucagon ↓, weight ↓
  • DPP-4 inhibitors: Inhibit GLP-1/GIP degradation; weight neutral
  • SGLT2 inhibitors: Block renal glucose reabsorption → glycosuria; DKA risk
  • Thiazolidinediones: PPAR-γ agonism → insulin sensitizer; edema, HF risk
🩸 Anticoagulant Mechanisms & Antidotes
  • Heparin (UFH): ↑ Antithrombin III → ↓ IIa + Xa | Antidote: Protamine
  • LMWH (enoxaparin): ↑ AT III → mainly anti-Xa | Partial reversal: Protamine
  • Warfarin: Blocks Vit K reductase → ↓ II,VII,IX,X | Antidote: Vit K + FFP
  • Dabigatran: Direct thrombin inhibitor | Antidote: Idarucizumab
  • Apixaban/Rivaroxaban: Direct factor Xa inhibitor | Antidote: Andexanet alfa
  • Monitor: UFH → aPTT; Warfarin → PT/INR; DOACs → usually none
🔥 High-Yield Drug Toxicities
  • Aminoglycosides: Nephrotoxicity + Ototoxicity (monitor peaks/troughs)
  • Vancomycin: Red Man Syndrome (slow infusion), nephrotoxicity
  • Methotrexate: Myelosuppression, hepatotoxicity → rescue with Leucovorin
  • Amiodarone: Thyroid, pulmonary, hepatic, corneal microdeposits, photosensitivity
  • Clozapine: Agranulocytosis (mandatory ANC monitoring)
  • Isotretinoin: Teratogenic (Category X) → iPLEDGE REMS required
  • Fluoroquinolones: Tendon rupture, QT prolongation, avoid <18 y/o

🏷️ FDA Approval & Recall Classifications
📋 NDA vs ANDA vs BLA
  • NDA: New Drug Application — Phase I/II/III clinical data required (novel drug)
  • ANDA: Abbreviated NDA — Generic drug; bioequivalence (AUC/Cmax within 80–125%) instead of full trials
  • BLA: Biologics License Application — Biologics (vaccines, mAbs, blood products)
  • 510(k): Medical device "substantial equivalence" clearance
  • Orange Book: Lists FDA-approved drugs + therapeutic equivalence codes (AB = bioequivalent)
⚠️ Drug Recall Classes
  • Class I: Serious adverse health consequences or DEATH likely — immediate action
  • Class II: May cause temporary/reversible health consequences; remote risk of serious harm
  • Class III: Unlikely to cause adverse health consequences; violation of FDA regulations
  • Market Withdrawal: Minor violation; company removes product voluntarily
  • Medical Device Safety Alert: Serious injury possible but not yet recall-level
🛡️ REMS Programs — Key Examples
  • iPLEDGE: Isotretinoin — monthly pregnancy test, 2 contraceptives
  • THALOMID REMS: Thalidomide — risk of severe birth defects
  • TOUCH (Natalizumab): PML risk from JC virus reactivation
  • Clozapine REMS: ANC monitoring for agranulocytosis
  • TIRF REMS: Transmucosal fentanyl — opioid addiction/overdose risk
🏥 Pharmacy Practice Laws
  • PPPA 1970: Child-resistant packaging (exceptions: nitro SL, oral contraceptives, patient waiver)
  • HIPAA: PHI protected; treatment exception allows provider-to-provider sharing
  • DQSA 2013: Track-and-trace supply chain (DSCSA); 503B outsourcing facilities
  • PDMPs: State databases tracking controlled substance dispensing
  • MMA 2003: Created Medicare Part D; required MTM programs
🚨 FPGEE Exam Trap: Durham-Humphrey defines Rx drugs (1951). Kefauver-Harris added EFFICACY requirement (1962). The CSA created DEA schedules (1970) — NOT the FD&C Act. OBRA '90 = counseling for Medicaid; MMA 2003 = MTM for Medicare Part D.
📐 Essential Pharmacokinetic Formulas
Clearance: CL = Dose / AUC (or CL = Vd × ke)
Volume of Dist: Vd = Dose / C₀ (or Vd = CL / ke)
Half-life: t½ = 0.693 / ke = 0.693 × Vd / CL
Steady State: Css = (F × Dose/τ) / CL [~4–5 t½ to reach]
Loading Dose: LD = Cp(target) × Vd / F
Maintenance: MD = Css × CL × τ / F
Bioavailability: F = AUCoral / AUCiv × 100%
Renal CL: CLr = (fu × GFR) + Tubular Secretion − Reabsorption
Cockcroft-Gault: CrCl = [(140−age) × wt(kg)] / [72 × SCr] × 0.85(♀)

🧠 High-Yield FPGEE Mnemonics
Antibiotic Ribosome Targets

30S vs 50S Inhibitors

BIG MAC
  • Buy — Bind 30S: Aminoglycosides
  • It — Inhibit 30S: Tetracyclines
  • Get — Grouped 50S: Macrolides
  • More — More 50S: Chloramphenicol
  • And — And 50S: Clindamycin
  • Cheaper — Clindamycin + Linezolid = 50S
Vancomycin Side Effects

Opioid Side Effects

Effects to Remember

STRAP
  • Sedation / euphoria
  • Tolerance develops (not to constipation!)
  • Respiratory depression (main cause of death)
  • Antitussive effect
  • Pinpoint pupils (miosis, no tolerance)
  • + Constipation (no tolerance) + Nausea
Beta-Blocker Contraindications

When to AVOID Beta-Blockers

ABCDE
  • Asthma / Reactive Airway Disease (non-selective)
  • Bradycardia / Heart Block (2° or 3°)
  • Cardiogenic Shock
  • Diabetes (masks hypoglycemia signs; except sweating)
  • Erection dysfunction (caution, not strict CI)
Amiodarone Toxicities

Multi-Organ Toxicity

THYPL
  • Thyroid dysfunction (hyper OR hypo)
  • Hepatic toxicity (LFTs)
  • Yellow skin (photosensitivity, blue-gray skin)
  • Pulmonary toxicity (fibrosis)
  • Lens/cornea microdeposits (ophthalmic exam)
ACEI Side Effects

ACE Inhibitor Adverse Effects

CAPTOPRIL
  • Cough (bradykinin ↑)
  • Angioedema ⚠️
  • Proteinuria decrease (beneficial)
  • Teratogenicity (Cat D; avoid in pregnancy)
  • Ortho-static hypotension (1st dose)
  • Potassium increase (hyperkalemia)
  • Rash / taste alteration
  • Increase Renin
  • Lowered GFR initially
H. Pylori Triple Therapy

Standard Treatment Regimen

OAC / MAC
  • Omeprazole (PPI) — suppress acid
  • Amoxicillin — cell wall inhibitor
  • Clarithromycin — protein synthesis inhibitor
  • Duration: 10–14 days
  • Alternative (high resistance areas): Bismuth Quadruple Therapy (PBMT)
Serotonin Syndrome vs NMS

Key Distinguishing Features

Serotonin = FAST
  • S: Serotonin Syndrome = Rapid onset (<24h)
  • F: Flushing, Fever, Diarrhea, Hyperthermia
  • A: Agitation, Hyperreflexia, Clonus (key!)
  • S: SSRI + MAOi cause it
  • T: Treat: Cyproheptadine (5-HT2A antagonist)
  • NMS = Slow onset, Rigidity, No clonus, Dopamine cause
